Charles Townshend

Before Kut

Before the Seige of Kut Charles Townhsend is described as ambitious. Townshend spent the first seven months of WWI in India, he was then put in charge of the 6th Division. His division won many victories inculding: Amara, Es Sinn, Nasiriyah, and Kut. He did not want to advance to Baghdad, because he thought is would result in a loss. General Sir John Nixon, who was pressured by the war cabinent ordered Townshend to attack Baghdad.

Siege of Kut

When marching at Baghdad Townshend was held back at Kut. Townshend stayed in Kut and waited for help from Basra, and to rest his wounded men. Townshend asked for a relif force to come help, but the forces were stopped by the Ottomans. Then the British army attempted to drop supplies, but those were intercepted by the Ottomans. Desise was prominent during the siege, and the relief force had suffered 23,000 losses. Townshend surrendered in Kut on 29 April 1916.

Interesting Facts

At first Townshend was praised as the "Hero of Kut"

After the war he released memoirs titled My Campaign in Mesopotamia

He was elected into Parliament as the Independent Conservative

After it was found how the POWs were treated his reputation declined
